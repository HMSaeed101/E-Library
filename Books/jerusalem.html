Description

Jerusalem: The Biography by Simon Sebag Montefiore is a comprehensive history of the city, spanning over 3,000 years from its ancient origins under King David through to the modern era. It chronicles how this remote town became the holy epicenter for Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, serving as a universal city, the capital of two peoples, the shrine of three faiths, and a perpetual site of conflict, empire-building, faith, slaughter, fanaticism, and coexistence. The book explores the lives of key figures who shaped it—from biblical kings and prophets to conquerors like Alexander the Great, Saladin, and modern leaders—while examining its role in the birth of major religions, the Israel-Palestine conflict, and the forging of the Middle East, portraying Jerusalem as the only city that exists twice: in heaven and on earth
