Description

Great Expectations follows the life of Pip, an orphan boy who grows up in poverty but later receives mysterious financial support that allows him to enter high society. Through his journey, he experiences love, pride, shame, and moral growth. The novel explores themes of social class, personal identity, ambition, and redemption. Dickens uses Pip’s story to show how wealth and status do not always lead to happiness or moral strength. Considered one of Dickens’ greatest works, the novel provides a deep picture of Victorian society while telling an emotional story about personal growth and human values.
