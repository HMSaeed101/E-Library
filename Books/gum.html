Description

The Fates of Human Societies is a Pulitzer Prize-winning book by Jared Diamond that explains why Eurasian civilizations (not Eurasians) have survived and conquered others. It argues against the idea that Eurasian hegemony is due to any form of Eurasian intellectual, moral, or inherent genetic superiority. Instead, Diamond posits that gaps in power and technology between human societies originate primarily in environmental differences, which are amplified by various positive feedback loops. When Europeans reached sub-Saharan Africa in the late Middle Ages, indigenous armies maintained a substantial military superiority; but, although these climatic advantages lasted even after sub-Saharan colonization by Europeans, Diamond argues that these small differences, with the same ultimate cause, were amplified and led to the observed outcome differences
