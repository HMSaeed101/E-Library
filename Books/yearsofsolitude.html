Description

The book tells the story of seven generations of the Buendía family living in the fictional Latin American town of Macondo. Through the rise and fall of this family and their town, the novel reflects about 100 years of social, political, and cultural change in Latin America. The narrative follows the founding of Macondo by José Arcadio Buendía and his wife Úrsula, then slowly shows how the town grows, becomes connected to the outside world, and eventually collapses. Strange but ordinary-feeling events happen, like ghosts living among people, characters ascending to heaven, or plagues affecting memory. This blending of reality with fantasy is the signature style of magical realism, where supernatural events are treated as normal parts of life.
