Description

The Muqaddimah, often translated as "Introduction" or "Prolegomenon," is a seminal work written by the 14th-century Arab historian Ibn Khaldun in 1377, originally intended as the prologue to his larger historical text, Kitab al-ʿIbar. It presents a comprehensive view of universal history, critiquing common errors made by historians and exploring the challenges of historical scholarship. Widely regarded as the most important Islamic history of the premodern world, it lays foundational ideas in fields like sociology, economics, political theory, Islamic theology, and even the natural sciences such as biology and chemistry. Key concepts include the cyclical rise and fall of civilizations driven by 'asabiyyah' (social cohesion) and environmental factors, making it a pioneering text in social sciences.
